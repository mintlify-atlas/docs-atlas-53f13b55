The `write-seo-geo-content` skill writes product-led content pages that rank in search engines and earn citations from AI engines like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews. It operates in two modes — Planning Mode for defining article strategy, and Writing Mode for drafting a confirmed topic — and never writes a single word of body content before completing pre-writing research. No fabricated statistics, no unverified sources, no skipped steps.

## What it produces

A single markdown file per article with YAML frontmatter at the top and pure content below. The file is ready to import directly into any CMS.


## Usage

Invoke from the chat interface:

```
/write-seo-geo-content
```

The skill asks whether you need Planning Mode (defining topics) or Writing Mode (drafting a confirmed topic). In Writing Mode, it completes all four pre-writing research steps — reference review, page brief, image research, and content research — before drafting.

## Content methodology

Every page is written to satisfy both search intent and AI answer extraction simultaneously. The skill starts from the target GEO prompt, not just the keyword, and structures content so that key passages can be lifted and cited by AI systems without surrounding context.

Core principles:
- **Answer first** — the lead is never buried. Readers and AI extraction both need the answer in the first screen.
- **No fabricated data** — if a statistic cannot be verified with a source URL, it is not used.
- **Balanced perspective** — comparisons acknowledge where the product loses, not just where it wins. This is what makes comparison pages trustworthy to both search engines and AI engines.
- **Brand mention mechanism** — every article has an explicit, defensible reason why the brand belongs in the AI answer for the target prompt.

## The 5 content zones

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Direct Answer Block (40–60 words)">
    The Quick Answer block is an H2 phrased as a question ("What is [Topic]?") followed by a direct definition in 1–3 sentences, under 60 words total. It is optimized for Google featured snippets and AI citation extraction.

    **Format:**
    ```
    What is [Topic]?
    [Topic] is [concise definition]. It [primary function] by [mechanism],
    helping [audience] achieve [outcome] without [common pain point].
    ```

    Requirements:
    - Under 60 words total including the heading
    - Contains at least 1 specific number where the topic supports it
    - Stands alone completely without surrounding context — an AI engine must be able to quote it on its own
    - Placed within the first third of the article
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Comparison Table (5+ criteria)">
    Comparison pages lead with a table comparing the solution against alternatives across 4–6 concrete, verifiable criteria. For high-value articles, the `create-geo-charts` skill can render this as a visual comparison chart with the full GEO text layer.

    Requirements:
    - Minimum 5 evaluation criteria
    - All values must be verifiable, not editorial claims
    - Balanced — include criteria where the product is not the winner. Lopsided tables signal bias to both human readers and AI systems.
    - Followed by a clear verdict ("Best for X if...") that is independently quotable
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Data and Evidence Section">
    The problem/context block names the pain point explicitly with 2–3 cited statistics that quantify its scope or cost. The evidence block in the comparison or solution section supports every capability claim with a named source.

    Requirements:
    - Minimum 5 data points per article across the full piece
    - Every number linked inline to its primary source URL
    - Sources from `.gov`, `.edu`, research institutions, or named industry publications
    - Named attribution inline: "According to [Source Name]..." not "Studies show..."
    - If the problem has quantitative data worth visualizing, invoke `create-geo-charts` for a chart embedded with its text summary, HTML data table, and JSON-LD
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Scenario Solutions (min 3 scenarios)">
    The Use Cases / Scenarios block covers at least 3 distinct scenarios for different audience segments. Each scenario follows the same format:

    ```
    **[Specific Role at Specific Company Type]**: [Problem they face]
    → [How solution helps] → [Specific outcome or metric]
    ```

    Requirements:
    - Minimum 3 distinct scenarios per page when the page type calls for scenarios
    - Named specific roles (e.g., "Head of Content at a 30-person B2B SaaS company")
    - At least 1 data point or metric per scenario where available
    - 300–400 words total for this section
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="FAQ Section (6–8 questions)">
    Not every page needs an FAQ. Include one when the page targets question-style searches, users naturally have objections about the topic, or the page covers a complex category or comparison. Skip it when the body already answers the likely questions.

    When included:
    - 3–6 specific questions targeting real "People Also Ask" queries for the keyword
    - At least 1 question phrased like the target GEO prompt if it fits naturally
    - Each answer: 2–4 sentences, complete and standalone without surrounding context
    - Cover pricing questions, comparison questions ("vs. [competitor]"), and how-to questions
    - Add FAQPage JSON-LD schema markup for the section
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Structured data

The skill adds JSON-LD structured data appropriate to the page type. All four types below come directly from the SKILL.md specification.

**FAQPage** — added to any page with an FAQ section:

```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "FAQPage",
  "mainEntity": [
    {
      "@type": "Question",
      "name": "What is generative engine optimization?",
      "acceptedAnswer": {
        "@type": "Answer",
        "text": "Generative engine optimization (GEO) is the practice of structuring content so AI engines like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews cite it in their answers."
      }
    }
  ]
}
```

**HowTo** — added to guide and workflow pages:

```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "HowTo",
  "name": "How to Get Cited in ChatGPT Responses",
  "step": [
    {
      "@type": "HowToStep",
      "name": "Write a direct answer block",
      "text": "Open every article with a 40–60 word answer block that stands alone as a quotable unit."
    }
  ]
}
```

**Product / ItemList** — added to comparison and money pages to expose the product or a ranked list to AI engines.

**ClaimReview** — added when the page explicitly fact-checks a claim in the industry, increasing trustworthiness signals for both search and AI engines.

## GEO optimization techniques

These techniques are drawn from peer-reviewed research on AI citation behavior. The skill applies them on every article.

| Technique | Citation lift | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Quotations from authoritative sources | +41% AI visibility | GEO paper, KDD 2024 |
| Statistics and data points inline | +33% AI visibility | GEO paper, KDD 2024 |
| Cite sources inline with named attribution | +28% AI visibility | GEO paper, KDD 2024 |
| Answer-first structure | 44.2% of ChatGPT citations come from the first 30% of content | SE Ranking analysis, 2025 |

The skill also applies E-E-A-T signals throughout: analytical language and real-world scenarios for Experience; correct use of industry terminology and trade-off acknowledgment for Expertise; high-authority external citations for Authoritativeness; and verifiable claims with balanced perspective for Trustworthiness.

<Tip>
Articles over 2,900 words average 5.1 AI citations compared to 3.2 for articles under 800 words (SE Ranking, 2025). The skill targets a minimum of 2,500 words per article and 3,000+ for comparison and guide pages.
</Tip>
