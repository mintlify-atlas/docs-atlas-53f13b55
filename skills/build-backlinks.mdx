title: 'Find and build backlinks for AI and search visibility'

---

This skill finds free, high-impact opportunities to get a brand mentioned and linked across the web — not mass email outreach or link farms, but showing up where AI engines train and retrieve from. The output is a ready-to-execute action plan: specific URLs, scored and prioritized, with draft responses written for each opportunity.

## Why this matters for GEO

AI engines (ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Gemini) cite brands that appear across diverse, authoritative sources. A single mention on a high-traffic Hacker News thread or a well-trafficked Quora answer can trigger AI citation. Unlike traditional SEO backlinking, GEO backlinks are about presence in the sources AI engines index and quote from — not volume, but authority and relevance.

## What it produces

A `backlink_plan.md` file organized by time horizon:

- **Quick Wins** — high impact, low effort (do this week)
- **High Value** — high impact, medium effort (do this month)
- **Long Play** — high impact, high effort (plan for later)
- **Recurring actions** — weekly and monthly cadence to maintain presence

Every item includes a direct URL, a one-line rationale, a time estimate, and a ready-to-post draft.

## High-value sources for AI citation

| Source | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Hacker News | Heavily indexed by AI engines; high domain authority |
| Quora | Frequently cited in AI-generated answers |
| Wikipedia | Highest authority; strictest editorial rules |
| GitHub | Discussions, awesome-lists, and READMEs cited by technical AI tools |
| Stack Overflow / Stack Exchange | Technical authority for developer-facing brands |
| Industry forums and communities | Category-specific authority and relevance |
| Free directories (G2, Capterra, Product Hunt) | Structured brand data AI engines use for comparisons |
| Dev.to, Medium, Hashnode | Syndication platforms with strong AI indexing |

## Usage

Invoke with `/build-backlinks`. The skill requires `brand_dna.md` (from the research-brand skill) and optionally uses published content URLs and `geo_prompt_targets.md`:

```
/build-backlinks
```

If `brand_dna.md` does not exist, the skill will ask you to run `/research-brand` first.

<Note>
Every opportunity in the output must have a real, clickable URL. No generic advice like "post on Reddit" — the skill finds the actual thread.
</Note>

## 5-phase process

<Steps>
  <Step title="Gather context">
    Read the available input files to understand what the brand does, who the competitors are, what content already exists, and which GEO prompts are being targeted.

    Then ask the user:
    - Which channels are you already active on? (to skip those)
    - Any channels to avoid?
    - What is your time budget? (e.g., 30 min/week, 2 hours/week)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Channel research">
    Search each relevant platform for existing conversations about the brand's category. For each channel, collect: specific URLs, recency, whether competitors are mentioned, whether the brand is already mentioned, and estimated audience size (upvotes, views, followers).

    Search patterns used:
    - **Hacker News** — `site:news.ycombinator.com "[category]"`, `"Show HN" [category]`, `"Ask HN" [related problem]`
    - **Quora** — `site:quora.com "best [product type]"`, `"[competitor]" OR "[category]"`, `"how to [problem brand solves]"`
    - **GitHub** — `site:github.com "awesome-[category]"`, `"[category]" list OR resource OR tools`
    - **Directories** — `"[category]" directory submit`, `"[competitor]" site:g2.com OR site:capterra.com`
    - **Wikipedia** — `site:wikipedia.org "[category]"` to find articles where the brand could be mentioned
  </Step>
  <Step title="Score and prioritize">
    Each opportunity is scored on three factors and assigned a priority level.

    | Factor | Weight | Description |
    |--------|--------|-------------|
    | GEO Impact | 40% | How likely AI engines are to cite this source |
    | Effort | 30% | How much time and skill the action requires |
    | Relevance | 30% | How closely the thread matches the brand's category and target prompts |

    **Priority levels:**
    - **Quick Win** — high impact, low effort; do this week
    - **High Value** — high impact, medium effort; do this month
    - **Long Play** — high impact, high effort; plan for later
    - **Skip** — low impact or disproportionate effort
  </Step>
  <Step title="Draft actionable responses">
    For every Quick Win and High Value opportunity, write a ready-to-post response or submission. See response rules below.

    For directory and listing submissions, the draft includes: listing copy (title, description, category, tags) and the submission URL with any requirements.

    For Hacker News "Show HN" posts, the draft includes: post title, description, the best content or tool to share, and the optimal posting time (weekday mornings US time).

    For GitHub awesome-list PRs, the draft includes: the PR description and one-line entry, with a link to the list's contribution guidelines.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build the action plan">
    Compile everything into `workspace/[customer-name]/backlink_plan.md`, organized by Quick Wins, High Value, Long Play, and Recurring Actions. Each entry includes a direct URL, rationale, time estimate, action, and full draft text.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Response rules

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Be genuinely helpful first">
    Every response must add value even without the brand mention. Answer the question, share an insight, or provide data. The response should be worth reading on its own.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Mention the brand naturally">
    Do not lead with the brand. Weave it in as one option among several, or as supporting evidence for a point being made. The brand is a recommendation, not the subject of the response.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Match the channel's tone">
    Hacker News values technical depth and dislikes marketing speak. Quora expects structured, detailed answers. Reddit communities have their own norms. Adjust the writing style to fit each platform.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Disclose affiliation where appropriate">
    On Hacker News and similar communities where transparency is expected, include a note like "Full disclosure: I work with [brand]." This builds trust and follows community norms.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Never be spammy">
    If you cannot draft a response that is genuinely helpful, mark the opportunity as "Skip — no natural fit" rather than forcing a mention. Spammy responses damage brand reputation and get removed.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Link to specific content, not the homepage">
    Every link should point to the specific article, guide, or resource that directly answers the thread's question. The homepage link is rarely the right target.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Recurring actions

The action plan includes a recurring cadence to maintain brand presence over time:

| Cadence | Action | Channel |
|---------|--------|---------|
| Weekly | Check for new "Ask HN" / "Show HN" about the category | Hacker News |
| Weekly | Check for new questions about the category | Quora |
| Monthly | Search for new category directory listings | Directories |
| Monthly | Check for new awesome-lists in the category | GitHub |

The skill generates the specific search queries and alert configurations for each cadence item based on the brand's category and competitor landscape.
